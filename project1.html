Working Pulse Oximeter

A pulse oximeter is a non-invasive device that measures the oxygen saturation level in the blood. It works by passing a beam of light through the skin and measuring the amount of light absorbed by oxygenated and deoxygenated blood.

Image

Features

  • Real-time Oxygen Saturation (SpO₂) Measurement
  • Real-time Pulse Rate (Heart BPM) Monitoring
  • High Accuracy using Red and Infrared Sensors
  • Digital Display of Readings (LED)
  • Low Power Consumption
  • Safe and Non-invasive Measurement
  • Built-in Power Supply (Battery Operated)
  • Compact and Portable Design

How it works

  • Red and Infrared LEDs shine light through the fingertip.
  • Blood absorbs different amounts of red and infrared light depending on oxygen levels.
  • A sensor detects how much light passes through the finger.
  • The analog light signals are converted into electrical signals.
  • Arduino processes the electrical signals.
  • The final SpO₂ and pulse rate values are displayed on a screen or serial monitor.

Components

  • Arduino UNO
  • MAX30100 Sensor
  • Breadboard
  • 16x2 LCD Display
  • Potentiometer
  • 9V Battery
  • Jumper wires
